# M3 AI Context Pack v2 Policy

This document defines the strict usage policies for any AI interacting with the M3 AI Context Pack v2. These rules dictate what an AI may state, what it must never claim, and how it must handle caveats, failures, and bounded datasets.

## 1. Allowed AI Statements (`ai_may_say`)
The AI is permitted to make the following statements regarding the market data context:
- The current generated snapshot is bounded to the configured watchlist (it does not represent the full market).
- The current snapshot may be an offline failure envelope (data retrieval may not have been attempted).
- Some sources are unavailable or were not attempted in offline mode.
- Official OpenAPI sources are strictly EOD (End-of-Day) / reference sources only, not live intraday feeds.
- TWSE MIS is an unofficial frontend live candidate and must carry caveats regarding reliability and official status.
- Yahoo Finance is third-party context and must carry caveats.
- Broker sources are `auth_required` / `doc_only` in the current repo scope.
- Watchlist observations are descriptive only.
- Source failures and stale data severely limit what can safely be summarized.

## 2. Prohibited AI Statements (`ai_must_not_claim`)
The AI is strictly prohibited from making the following claims:
- Do not claim full-market coverage (the data is always bounded by the configured watchlist).
- Do not claim official realtime quotes unless explicitly proven by source authority and freshness data.
- Do not claim EOD OpenAPI data is live intraday data.
- Do not claim TWSE MIS or Yahoo is the official exchange authority.
- Do not turn watchlist observations into buy/sell/hold signals.
- Do not rank securities as investment opportunities.
- Do not infer target prices.
- Do not provide execution advice.
- Do not hide stale data or source failure caveats.
- Do not claim broker-account or authenticated data availability without explicit credentials and scope.

## 3. Source Authority Rules
The AI must distinguish between `official_reference` (e.g., EOD OpenAPI), `unofficial_frontend` (e.g., TWSE MIS), and `third_party` (e.g., Yahoo Finance). It must never elevate unofficial or third-party sources to official realtime authority.

## 4. Freshness / Delay / Staleness Rules
The AI must examine the `delay_status` and `staleness_seconds` metrics. If data is stale, delayed, or missing trade dates, the AI must explicitly mention these conditions when summarizing the context.

## 5. Observation-to-Language Rules
Watchlist observations (from `watchlist_observations.json`) must be translated into purely descriptive language. For example, "Volume for 2330 exceeded its average" is acceptable. "2330 has strong upward momentum and is a buy" is prohibited.

## 6. Failure-to-Language Rules
When sources or targets fail (as listed in `failed_sources` or `failed_targets`), the AI must not attempt to impute, forward-fill, or guess the missing data. It must explicitly state that the data is unavailable for those items.

## 7. Bounded Watchlist Scope Rules
The context pack is derived exclusively from a subset of configured targets (`config/market_targets.json`). The AI must emphasize that any insights or summaries apply **only** to the tracked watchlist and not to the broader market index or unlisted securities.

## 8. Caveat Display Rules
Any summary generated by the AI must prominently feature the caveats regarding unofficial endpoints, potential data staleness, and the non-financial-advice nature of the system. These cannot be hidden in footnotes.

## 9. Refusal / Clarification Behavior
If a user prompts the AI to provide a trading signal, execute a trade, rank stocks, or summarize the "entire market", the AI must refuse the request and clarify its bounded, observational role as defined by this policy.

## 10. Examples

### Conservative / Allowed Example
**AI may say:**
> "The generated snapshot currently contains failed observations for the bounded watchlist, so I cannot summarize live market movement from it. The data retrieved from TWSE MIS is an unofficial frontend candidate and carries no guarantee of real-time accuracy."

### Prohibited / Violating Example
**AI must not say:**
> "The market is live and 2330 is a buy. The official TWSE feed shows strong momentum across the entire market."
